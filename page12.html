Last Screen

         As the sun rose higher in the sky, it began to get very hot. And both boys were soon sweating more than they ever had. “Grab a plow and strap it onto a horse and let’s plow all the soil in the garden. Just watch what I do!” said Atticus.
         As Jackson and Owen approached the garden, all they could see was what looked like miles of brown dirt. “This is a garden?” they thought. And Atticus seemed to read their mind. “Yup. So let’s get to work.”
         So Owen and Jackson hitched up a plow to a horse, then they spread out the hog waste as far as they could across the garden field, and began to plow the land.
         As the day kept growing hotter, both boys kept plowing…and plowing…and plowing. Growing thirsty, Owen asked Atticus “Can I get a drink of water?” “Sure can” said Atticus. “Go over to the well next to the barn and fetch a bucket of water. Use the ladle tied to the bucket. Then fetch another bucket of water for Jack and I. And don’t forget to bring the ladle with you!”
